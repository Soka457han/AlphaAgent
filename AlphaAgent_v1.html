① Vol Sweet-Spot Logic
Volatility is the edge gate
Crypto dislocations only revert reliably when annualized vol sits between 15–40%. Below 15% — market is directionless, no dislocation exists. Above 40% — dislocations become trends, mean-reversion fails. This band is not arbitrary: it represents the regime where temporary fear creates exploitable overshoot without structural breakdown.
② RSI Mean-Reversion Assumption
Oversold = temporary, not terminal
When RSI-14 drops below 28 on a liquid spot asset inside the vol band, it signals panic selling beyond fundamental value. Liquid assets — BTC, ETH, BNB, SOL — have persistent institutional bid support that absorbs oversold dislocations within days. Exit when RSI crosses back above 60 — the mean-reversion move has played out.

What Worked, What Didn't, and Why the Thesis Survives

This is a first-version parameter problem, not a thesis problem. Here is the honest breakdown.

✓ What worked
  • · Capital protection: −2.9% max DD while BTC fell 44%. The vol harness did exactly what it was designed to do.
  • · Thesis validated: Strategy correctly identified that Dec 2025–Jun 2026 was a trending bear market, not a mean-reversion regime — and stayed cash.
  • · Regime filter worked: Extreme fear correctly triggered majors-only mode, blocking altcoin entries during the worst crashes.
  • · 4-exit system: No position became a runaway loss — all 3 trades exited within the designed risk parameters.
✗ Why only 3 round trips?
  • · Vol band too narrow: 15–40% excludes most of the Dec–May period when crypto was in a sustained trend (vol consistently 40%+). The harness correctly blocked entry but left too few trade opportunities.
  • · Time stop too short: 7-day limit forced exits before some mean-reversions completed, contributing to the negative avg round trip.
  • · Entries in late-trend: The 3 trades that fired (Jun 18–27) caught assets still in downtrend — the RSI recovered to 28 threshold but the trend wasn't done. Need ADX confirmation to avoid trend-disguised-as-dip entries.
  • · Win rate 33%: 1 of 3 trades worked. Small sample but a signal that entry timing needs refinement.
→ v15 Planned Improvements
  • · Widen vol band 10–55% → more trade opportunities while staying within manageable risk
  • · Extend time stop 7→14 days → let mean-reversion complete its natural cycle
  • · Add ADX confirmation → ADX >25 = trending market, block entry. ADX <25 = ranging, permit entry. This is the missing filter.
  • · Switch to 4H bars → daily bars create too-slow reaction on fast reversions; 4H gives 6× more signal resolution
  • · Real F&G API → replace price-derived proxy with Alternative.me live data for more accurate regime detection
Honest verdict: The vol harness idea is structurally sound — it protected capital during one of crypto's sharpest 6-month drawdowns. The problem is parameterisation: 15–40% was too narrow a band for the Dec 2025–Jun 2026 regime, generating only 3 trips. 3 trades is not enough data to evaluate a strategy. The v15 improvements above directly address every failure mode identified. The core assumption — that vol-sweet-spot + RSI-oversold = exploitable mean-reversion — remains worth refining, not abandoning.
